Pufferfishes

Pufferfish can inflate into a ball shape to evade predators. Also
known as blowfish, these clumsy swimmers fill their elastic
stomachs with huge amounts of water (and sometimes air) and
blow themselves up to several times their normal size.

Korea, China and Japan consider puffer fish a culinary delicacy and
only specially-trained chefs know how to serve them safely. In the
wild, puffer fish are predators, and eat a variety of snails, shellfish,
crustaceans and other fish. In captivity, puffers will eat almost anything,
so a variety of foods should be offered to allow for a healthy mix.
